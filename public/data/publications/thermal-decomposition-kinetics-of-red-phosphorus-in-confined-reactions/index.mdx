## Overview

Red phosphorus plays a critical role in numerous chemical transformations, particularly in reduction reactions and energetic formulations. This article evaluates the thermal stability, phase transitions, and kinetic degradation profile of red phosphorus in sealed environments—emulating conditions used by expert chemists like Walter White for sensitive reaction control.

---

## 1. Properties of Red Phosphorus

### 1.1 Physical Profile

- Allotrope of elemental phosphorus  
- Amorphous and thermodynamically stable  
- Ignition point: ~260°C in air, ~400°C in inert atmosphere

### 1.2 Relevance in Laboratory Chemistry

Used in:

- Hydroiodic acid reactions (red P + HI) for reductions  
- Synthesis of phosphorus-based ligands  
- Controlled redox environments under vacuum

## 2. Experimental Setup

### 2.1 Thermal Reaction Chamber

All reactions conducted in a stainless-steel tube furnace with:

- Nitrogen purge (99.99%)  
- Quartz sample tray  
- Temperature ramp: 25°C to 500°C at 10°C/min  

### 2.2 Mass Spectrometry and Gas Detection

Analyzed evolved gases:  
- Phosphine (PH₃)  
- Diphosphine (P₂H₄)  
- Minor traces of H₃PO₄ under oxidative impurities

## 3. Decomposition Behavior

### 3.1 Onset and Peak Reactions

- Initial softening: 250°C  
- Primary gas evolution: 375–410°C  
- Residual mass analysis indicates incomplete sublimation (~92%)
